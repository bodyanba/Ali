Employers Must Correct Harassing Behavior
In California, employers have a legal duty to create a harassment-free work environment.
For many employers, this duty involves preventing foreseeable sexual harassment through proper training and the cultivation of a culture of respect for all individuals. This includes establishing an effective complaint or grievance process, providing regular anti-harassment training, and taking immediate and appropriate action when an employee complains. To be able to contribute to harassment-free workplaces, we have to be able to identify harassment when it happens. Are you able to identify harassing behavior when you see it?
